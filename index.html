I graduated from Marymount University in the Fall of 2019 with a degree in Interior Design. This page is meant to display my work and give you a taste for who I am. I chose to study interior design because it impacts every single person’s life, whether they realize it or not. A strategically curated interior is like magic, it can increase productivity, boost your mood and even improve your health. I chose to study interior design because I wanted to help change peoples lives, as cliché as that sounds. The backbone of every single one of my designs is empathy. I have a passion for people! Because of this, I seek to help define problems and create solutions in order to make interiors more beautiful, functional and accessible to all people.

Portfolio

Office Space

I collaborated to design an office space for Plenty, a young, vertical farming company with a purpose for providing fresh produce within cities. Their slogan is “produce for people and the planet”. Their passion for nature and produce is incorporated into the design through using patterns of biophilia to create a lush environment. The design ensures that the opportunity for collaboration and socialization through a centrally located conference room and huddle room. There are also areas for independent work with desking systems offering varying levels of privacy accommodating for all work preferences.




Dermatology Office

Canyons Dermatology & Skincare is a full range skincare center offering medical, surgical, cosmetic and spa services. The center is inspired by the stunning, vibrant layers of canyons, with each rock formation shaped over time to become unique in its beauty and form, similar to beauty and complexities of skin. This concept is incorporated into the design through the organic curves and layers of the floorplan and ceiling patterns, natural resin panels resembling sediment layers and warm pops of color throughout the space.




Labor and Delivery Suite

Ripple Luxury Labor and Delivery Suite was inspired by the concept of a stone hitting the water causing a ripple effect. Childbirth is much like a stone hitting the water, causing a ripple that launches into a continuous chain of ripples dancing across the waters surface. It is a momentous event that impacts nearly every part of that person’s life, from then on. This concept of the ripple is incorporated into the finishes with wavy lines simulating the pattern of a ripple in the water. Additionally, curved lines are used in the floor and ceiling to mimic waters organic movements. A calming palate of blues reflect the changing colors of water as the light bounces off the surface. The suite features a high-end birthing tub experience that allows for the mother to have the option for waterbirth. The custom wood headwall provides a hospitality feel and strategically hides the med gas equipment.




Modern Condo

This modern condo located in Alexandria, Virginia was designed with a bold, high end kitchen accommodating for a multi-generational family featuring lower counter space, cabinetry and wall appliances with heights that can be easily accessed by people of all ages. The furniture was carefully curated to provide stable arm support to allow for an easy transition to lift out of the seat. The bright red kitchen and artwork was designed to give visual interest and to stand out from the typical, tired look of grey, monochromatic interiors.




Villa Savoye

These are my interior interpretations of the Villa Savoy, designed by famous, French modernist architect Le Corbusier. I chose to create a mid-century design using warm tones, simple, modern furniture and bold geometric accents.
